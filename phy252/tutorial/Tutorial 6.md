a.
$$
	\sum_{i=1}^{n} p(i) = 1 \implies \sum_{i=1}^{n} A=1
$$
Therefore,
$$
	An=1\implies A=\frac{1}{n}
$$

b.
$$
	\left< \mathcal{S} \right> = \sum_{k=1}^{n} p(k) \ln\left( \frac{1}{p(k)} \right) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{n} \ln\left( \frac{1}{1 /n} \right)
$$
$$
	\sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{n} \ln n = \ln n
$$
---
c.
Definition of the mean:
$$
	\left< X \right> = \sum p(k)X(k)
$$
Therefore,
$$
	\left< x \right> = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{n}(k) = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^{n} k = \frac{1}{n} \frac{n(n+1)}{2} = \frac{n+1}{2}
$$
---
d.
$$
	\text{var}(x) = \left< x^{2} \right> - \left< x \right> ^{2}
$$
$$
	\left< x^{2} \right> = \sum_{k=1}^{n} p(k) x^{2}(k) = \sum_{k=1}^{n} \frac{1}{n} (k) ^{2} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^{n} k^{2}
$$
$$
	\left< x^{2} \right> = \frac{1}{n} \frac{(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} = \frac{(n+1)(2n+1)}{6n}
$$
The variance is therefore:
$$
	\text{var}(x) = \frac{(n+1)(2n+1)}{6n} - \left( \frac{n+1}{2} \right)^{2}
$$
