# Question 2
In an isothermal setting:
$$
	P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2} = P_{2}\left( \frac{V_{1}}{2} \right) \implies P_{1} = \frac{1}{2}P_{2}
$$
In an adiabatic setting:
$$
	P_{1}V_{1}^{\gamma} = P_{2}V_{2}^{\gamma} \implies P_{1}=\frac{1}{2^{\gamma}}P_{2}
$$
In the adiabatic setting, the final pressure $P_{2}$ is larger. Therefore, I conclude that a greater external pressure must be applied.
