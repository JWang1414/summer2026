# Question 2
---
a.
Definition of temperature,
$$
	T\equiv  \frac{2}{3k_{B}}\left< E_{K} \right>
$$
Where $\left< E_{K} \right>$ is the average kinetic energy per particle.
$$
	\Delta \left< E_{K} \right> = \frac{3}{2}k_{B} (\Delta T) \approx 2.07 \times 10^{-21} \text{ J}
$$
---
b.
This process is isothermal:
$$
	\Delta T=0 \implies \Delta \left< E \right> =0
$$
So there is no change in the average energy per particle.
# Question 3
---
a.
Energy in the pendulum ball:
$$
	U=mgh \approx 0.1(9.8)(1) = 0.98
$$
Ideal gas law:
$$
	PV = Nk_{B}T \implies N=\frac{PV}{k_{B}T} \approx 5.41 \times 10^{18}
$$
Where $P$, $V$, and $T$ refer to the initial pressure, volume, and temperature inside the box.

The energy transferred to the gas is:
$$
	\Delta E = NC_{V}\Delta T = N\left( \frac{5}{2} \right)k_{B}(\Delta T)
$$
$$
	\Delta T = \frac{2}{5} \frac{\Delta E}{Nk_{B}} \approx 5.25 \times 10^{3}
$$
Final temperature is,
$$
	T_{f} = 5.25 \times 10^{3} + 300 = 5.55 \times 10^{3}
$$
---
b.
At fixed volume, the ideal gas law gives,
$$
	P \propto T
$$
Therefore,
$$
	\frac{P_{f}}{P_{i}} = \frac{T_{f}}{T_{i}}
$$
The final pressure is,
$$
	P_{f} = P_{i} \frac{T_{f}}{T_{i}} = 18.5
$$
The change in pressure is $18.5-1=17.5$ pascals
