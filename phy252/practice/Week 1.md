c.
Potential energy to kinetic,
$$
	mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}
$$
$$
	v^{2}=2gh \implies v=\sqrt{ 2gh }
$$
Therefore,
$$
	v=\sqrt{ 2(9.8)(75) } \approx 38.34\text{ m/s}
$$

e.
Sum over the cloud as an integral.
$$
	\int_{0}^{1000} \int_{0}^{1000} \int_{1000}^{2000} mgy \, dy  \, dx  \, dz = mg (1000)^{2} \int_{1000}^{2000} y \, dy
$$
$$
	mg(1000)^{2}(1500000) \approx 7.34 \times 10^{15}
$$
A raindrop falling from this height would be moving at,
$$
	v=\sqrt{ 2gh } \approx \sqrt{ 2(9.8)(1000) } \approx 140 \text{ m/s}
$$
